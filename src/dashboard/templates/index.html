How to read this page

What's a "cluster"?

Customers are grouped into clusters using community detection on a graph of who shares an address or payment method with whom. Most clusters have just 1 customer (no shared connections found); a cluster with several accounts means those accounts are structurally linked, which is a stronger fraud signal than any one account's behavior alone.

What's the risk score?

A 0–1 score combining (a) how unusual each customer's return behavior is, scored by an Isolation Forest anomaly model, and (b) whether they belong to a larger, tightly-connected cluster. Higher = more suspicious.

Why this reason, not an item?

The model flags customer behavior patterns (return velocity, shared addresses, purchase-to-return timing) — not specific items. The sentence on each card explains which pattern(s) drove that score.
